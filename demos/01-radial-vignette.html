Radial Vignette

This technique uses a radial gradient that's slightly lighter in the center and gradually darkens toward the edges. It's one of the most conservative and widely-used approaches in modern web design.

The Effect

Look at the corners of your screen—notice how the background is subtly darker than the center? This creates a gentle vignette effect that:

  • Draws the eye toward the content
  • Adds depth without being noticeable
  • Works on all screen sizes
  • Has zero performance impact

The gradient transitions from a lighter blue (#013e5f) at the center to your base color (#013250) at 60%, then to a darker shade (#012240) at the edges.

The Code

background: radial-gradient( ellipse at center top, #013e5f 0%, #013250 60%, #012240 100% );

Why It Works

This technique is used by companies like Apple, Stripe, and Linear. It's subtle enough to be professional, yet noticeable enough to add visual polish. The ellipse shape at "center top" means the lighter area is positioned near your content's starting point.

Open this page and the baseline demo in side-by-side tabs. Toggle between them—you'll notice this version has more perceived depth and draws your attention to the content more naturally.

Best For

This is your safest, most conservative option. If you want to enhance the background without any risk of it being "too much," this is the way to go. It's sophisticated without being showy.
