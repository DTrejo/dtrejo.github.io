Layered Depth

This technique combines multiple background layers: a subtle accent gradient plus an SVG noise pattern. The layers create texture that's only visible when you look closely.

The Effect

Look at any solid area of the background—there's a very subtle texture, almost like paper grain or canvas. Combined with a faint cyan gradient at the top, it creates depth in two ways:

  • The noise adds tactile texture (zoom in to 150% to see it clearly)
  • The gradient provides a hint of your accent color (#63bbb9)
  • Both layers are ultra-low opacity (3-5%)
  • Creates richness without being obvious

The Code

background-color: #013250; background-image: radial-gradient( ellipse at top, rgba(99, 187, 185, 0.03) 0%, transparent 50% ), url('data:image/svg+xml;utf8,...'); /* SVG generates procedural noise */

Why It Works

The SVG fractal noise is generated in-browser (no external image file needed), making it extremely lightweight. The radial gradient adds a subtle aurora-like glow using your secondary accent color.

This approach is inspired by high-end editorial design where subtle texture implies quality and craftsmanship. It's invisible at first glance but adds richness when studied.

Best For

If you want texture that's only perceptible on close inspection. This is more sophisticated than a flat background but more subtle than obvious patterns. Works especially well on high-DPI displays where the texture becomes more refined.
