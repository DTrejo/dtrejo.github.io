Nebula Glow Zones

This technique creates localized color areas using heavily blurred radial gradients that slowly pulse and shift. It mimics the colorful gas clouds of nebulae—purple, cyan, indigo, and coral zones at extremely low opacity.

The Effect

Look at different areas of the background. You'll notice very subtle color tinting that varies by location. Four nebula zones are positioned across the viewport:

  • Cyan nebula (upper-left) — Your secondary accent color
  • Purple nebula (right side) — Deep space purple
  • Indigo nebula (bottom) — Dark violet
  • Coral nebula (center-right) — Your primary accent

Each zone slowly pulses over 20 seconds—growing slightly larger and brighter, then shrinking back. The 120px blur makes them diffuse like actual nebulae.

The Code

.nebula-glow { border-radius: 50%; filter: blur(120px); opacity: 0.05; background: radial-gradient( circle, rgba(99, 187, 185, 0.8) 0%, transparent 70% ); } @keyframes nebula-pulse { 0%, 100% { transform: scale(1); opacity: 0.05; } 50% { transform: scale(1.2); opacity: 0.08; } }

Why It Works

Real nebulae are massive clouds of ionized gas—they glow in specific colors based on which elements are present (hydrogen = red, oxygen = blue-green). By recreating this digitally:

  • Creates atmospheric depth through color variation
  • Ties your accent colors to astronomical phenomena
  • Adds warmth and life to flat blue background
  • Slow pulsing suggests organic, living cosmos

The extreme blur (120px) and low opacity (5-8%) means colors feel ambient rather than applied. Like colored light washing across a surface.

Color Theory

The chosen colors reference actual deep space photography:

  • Cyan/Turquoise: Oxygen III emissions in planetary nebulae
  • Purple/Violet: Combination of hydrogen-alpha and oxygen
  • Coral/Orange: Hydrogen-alpha emissions, dust reflection

These aren't arbitrary—they're scientifically accurate nebula colors that happen to match your design system.

Variations

You could:

  • Change nebula positions based on scroll depth
  • Use different colors per article category
  • Add more nebulae for denser atmosphere
  • Make movement respond to mouse position
  • Adjust opacity based on time of day

Best For

If you want color variety while maintaining dark aesthetic. This adds warmth and atmosphere without brightness—the background stays dark but gains personality. Perfect for sites where your brand includes multiple accent colors that deserve equal presence.
