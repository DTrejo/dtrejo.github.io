Lenticular Illusion

This technique creates a 3D depth illusion using vertical stripes that shift based on scroll position. It mimics lenticular printing (those images that change when you tilt them), creating perceived depth from 2D patterns.

The Effect

Scroll up and down slowly. Notice how the background stripes appear to shift and create a sense of depth—as if you're looking through venetian blinds or a ribbed surface. The pattern:

  • Uses your accent colors (cyan and coral)
  • Repeats every 40 pixels
  • Shifts perspective based on scroll position
  • Creates 3D illusion from 2D stripes
  • Extremely low opacity (3%)
↓ Scroll slowly to see the lenticular shift ↓

The Code

.lenticular-stripes { background: repeating-linear-gradient( 90deg, transparent 0px, rgba(99, 187, 185, 0.03) 10px, transparent 20px, rgba(245, 122, 89, 0.03) 30px, transparent 40px ); } // On scroll, apply subtle perspective shift window.addEventListener('scroll', () => { const scroll = window.pageYOffset; stripes.style.transform = `perspective(1000px) rotateY(${scroll * 0.01}deg) scale(1.05)`; });

Why It Works

Lenticular effects exploit parallax and perspective cues. Your brain interprets the shifting stripes as evidence of 3D depth—similar to how:

  • Lenticular prints show different images at different angles
  • Venetian blinds reveal/hide what's behind based on viewing angle
  • Parallax barriers in 3D displays direct different images to each eye

The subtle perspective transform creates motion parallax as you scroll, tricking your visual system into perceiving depth.

Perceptual Psychology

This technique leverages multiple depth cues:

  • Motion parallax: Stripes shift as you "move" (scroll)
  • Perspective: Subtle 3D transform suggests receding plane
  • Texture gradient: Regular stripe pattern implies surface
  • Interposition: Stripes appear to be "over" background

Calibration Warning

This effect requires careful tuning. Too much rotation/shift can cause:

  • Motion sickness (vestibular conflict)
  • Visual discomfort (straining to focus)
  • Distraction from content

Current settings (0.01deg per pixel scroll) are extremely subtle. Test thoroughly before production use.

Best For

If you want an experimental, almost psychedelic depth effect. This is the most "trippy" option—it plays with perception in noticeable ways. Best for creative/artistic sites or experimental portfolios. Not recommended for corporate/professional contexts where conventional design is expected.
